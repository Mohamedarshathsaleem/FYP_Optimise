Flow 1: Energy Data → Load Apportioning → SEU Flagging

Energy Data Management (meters & monthly GJ values)
        │
        │  user manually assigns GJ to equipment
        ▼
Load Apportioning (splits energy by equipment/SEU category)
        │
        │  system groups by SEU category, checks against threshold (e.g. ≥5%)
        ▼
SEU Flagging (flags significant energy users)
        │
        ▼
SEU Action Items (improvement actions for each flagged SEU)
In plain words: You enter monthly energy bills. Then in Load Apportioning, you manually break down where that energy goes (compressors, chillers, lighting, etc.). The system then flags any category using ≥5% of total energy as a "Significant Energy Use" and you attach action items to improve them.

Flow 2: Energy Data + Production → SEC Analysis

Energy Data Usages (monthly GJ)
Energy Resource Usages (monthly GJ)       ──► SEC Analysis (computed live, not stored)
Monthly Production Usages (monthly qty)   ──►   = Energy per unit of product
POE Allocations (user-set % per product)  ──►
In plain words: SEC = "how much energy per tonne of product?" The system takes your energy bills, your production quantities, and your POE allocation percentages, then calculates SEC in memory. Nothing is saved to a separate table — it reads live every time.

Flow 3: Energy Data + Variables → EIP Analysis

Energy Data Usages (monthly GJ)
Energy Resource Usages (monthly GJ)         ──► EIP Analysis (computed live, not stored)
Monthly Variable Usages (temp, humidity)     ──►   = Energy intensity vs variables
EIP Targets, Normalization Factors, Weather  ──►   + regression (R², slope, intercept)
In plain words: EIP = "how does energy relate to variables like temperature or production volume?" It plots energy vs. a variable, runs regression, and shows trends. Also computed live — no output table.

Key takeaways
Energy Data Management is the single source of truth — everything downstream reads from it
Load Apportioning is a manual step — the user decides how to split energy across equipment
SEU Flagging is the only module that auto-generates records from another module (Load Apportioning)
SEC and EIP are read-only analysis views — they compute on the fly and store nothing except user config (POE %, filter presets, targets)
